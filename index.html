I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Linguistics with a minor in Cognitive Science at Cornell University. I work on formal semantics and pragmatics with a focus on languages in the Himalayas & China.

Within semantics & pragmatics, I am interested in how languages express evidence, attitudes, epistemic commitments, and in verbal aspect and nominal reference. My work builds strongly on cross-linguistic data in a variety of formats, particularly from the Sino-Tibetan family.

I am also interested in semantic and syntactic change, especially from a formally rigorous perspective. I have worked on topics in Sino-Tibetan historical linguistics, particularly in the verbal and clausal domains.

At Cornell I am a member of the Language Documentation Lab and part of the East Asia and Southeast Asia Programs. Since 2014 I have been a research contributor to the Endangered Language Alliance in New York City.

I received my B.A. summa cum laude in Linguistics with concentration in Computer Science from Columbia University in 2016.
